- **Current Mitigation:** Patch-work with PENETRON compound (temporary)
- **Estimated Leakage Loss:** ~2-5% of total drawl (est. 50,000-125,000 m³/month unaccounted)

### Action Items:

**Immediate (Month 1-2):**
1. **Leak Audit & Quantification**
   - Conduct water balance study across all storage tanks, pipelines, and distribution network
   - Install water meters at main intake and major consumption points to identify anomalies
   - Estimated potential recovery: **50,000 m³/month** (2% of total) = **600,000 m³/year**
   - **Reduction in m³/tonne: 0.8-1.0 m³/tonne**

2. **Leakage Hot-spot Identification**
   - Priority areas: Chipper House (480,000), Wood Pulping-RDH (310,000), Paper Machines (395,000)
   - Conduct pressure testing on aged pipelines; replace corroded sections
   - Expected savings: **30,000-40,000 m³/month** from these sections alone

**Short-term (Month 3-6):**
3. **Permanent Leak Arresting**
   - Replace temporary PENETRON patches with structural repairs using:
     - Epoxy-lined tank coatings for long-term impermeability
     - High-pressure hydro-testing for pipeline integrity
     - Installation of automated leak detection systems with alarms
   - Targeted tanks: Clarified water storage, recycled water reservoirs
   - Expected savings: **25,000-35,000 m³/month**

**Long-term (Month 6-12):**
4. **Predictive Maintenance Program**
   - IoT-based water quality sensors at critical nodes
   - Pressure variance monitoring to detect micro-leaks
   - Annual maintenance protocol for high-risk sections
   - Expected sustained savings: **60,000-80,000 m³/month**

### Total Leakage Reduction Potential: **115,000-155,000 m³/month** (4.6-6.2% of total drawl)

## 3. HOT EFFLUENT DISCHARGE & HEAT RECOVERY + REUSE

- Volume: 300,000-350,000 m³/month at 75°C avg
- ΔT recovery: 45-55°C (cooling to 25-30°C for reuse)
- Thermal energy recoverable: **13-17 GJ/month equivalent to 3,600-4,700 kWh/month worth of steam**

### Action Items:

**Immediate (Month 1-4):**
1. **Comprehensive Heat Recovery Assessment**
   - Thermal imaging survey of all hot effluent streams
   - Identify temperature profiles and volume flows at each source
   - Expected findings: **60-80% of effluent suitable for recovery**, currently only **20-25% recovered**
   - **Recovery gap: 40,000-60,000 m³/month of hot water wasted**

2. **Expanded Plate Heat Exchanger Network**
   - Install additional PHE units at:
     - **Chemical Recovery outlet** → Heat DM water intake or pre-warm pulping water
     - **Bagasse Pulping discharge** → Preheat water for wet depithing process
     - **Paper Machine drainage** → Heat feed water to power boiler or recycled water
   - Efficiency gain: **10-15°C temperature elevation** of reused water
   - Expected cold fresh water displacement: **50,000-80,000 m³/month**
   - **Reduction in m³/tonne: 0.75-1.2 m³/tonne**

3. **Membrane-Based Treatment for Boiler Feedwater**
   - Hot effluent → Cooling (40-50°C) → MF/UF membrane treatment → RO polishing → DM exchange (hot side)
   - Reused as boiler feedwater / cooling tower makeup
   - Current DM water demand: 250,000 m³/month → Target reduction to 180,000 m³/month
   - **Expected savings: 70,000 m³/month** (displacement of DM water from river intake)
   - **Reduction in m³/tonne: 1.05 m³/tonne**

**Short-term (Month 5-12):**
4. **Industrial-Grade Wastewater Recycling System**
   - Deploy membrane bioreactor (MBR) for effluent treatment:
     - Input: 200,000-250,000 m³/month of mixed effluent
     - Output: 160,000-200,000 m³/month reusable water + 40,000-50,000 m³ waste stream
     - Membrane type: Submerged MF for robust operation with pulp/fiber content
   - Treated water reuse pathways:
     - 50% → Cooling tower makeup
     - 30% → Bagasse pulping process water
     - 20% → Domestic/sanitation after final polishing

5. **Advanced Oxidation & Polishing**
   - Post-MBR ultraviolet (UV) + ozone treatment for micro-contaminant removal

## 4. BAGASSE PULPING (WET DEPITHING) - CONTROLLED USAGE

### Current Baseline:
- **Total Bagasse Pulping (Unbleached + Bleached):** 91,000 m³/month
  - Unbleached: 40,000 m³ (44%)
  - Bleached: 51,000 m³ (56%)
- **Process:** Wet depithing consumes water-intensive operation for fiber separation
- **Current Efficiency:** 91,000 m³ per month usage

### Specific Issues:
1. **Excessive wash water in depithing section:** 40-50% of intake water not optimized
2. **High moisture content in bagasse input:** Increases water required
3. **Inefficient drainage:** Depithed material not adequately dewatered before storage

### Action Items:

**Immediate (Month 1-2):**
1. **Process Water Balance Study**
   - Detailed audit of water consumption in:
     - Depithing machine inlet water
     - Washing stages (primary, secondary, tertiary)
     - Screening and cleaning circuits
   - Current benchmark: 91,000 m³/month ÷ bagasse input tonnage = **specific water/kg bagasse**
   - Target reduction: **10-15% through optimization**
   - **Recovery potential: 9,000-13,500 m³/month**

2. **Inlet Bagasse Preprocessing**
   - Pre-dry bagasse feedstock (sun-drying or mechanical dewatering) before pulping
   - Reduces initial moisture load → Less water needed for depithing
   - Expected reduction: **8-12% of depithing water** = **7,000-11,000 m³/month**

**Short-term (Month 3-6):**
3. **Advanced Counter-Current Washing**
   - Implement counter-current detackifier stages:
     - Stage 1: Depither discharge water → Feed Stage 2 rinse
     - Stage 2: Clean water → Feed final wash
     - Recirculation percentage: **60-70%** of wash water reused
   - Expected water savings: **20,000-27,000 m³/month** (22-30% reduction from current 91,000)
   - **Reduction in m³/tonne: 0.3-0.4 m³/tonne**

4. **Dewatering Efficiency (High-Speed Screening)**
   - Install rotary vacuum screens or horizontal dewatering screens post-depithing
   - Removes moisture before pulping → Less water needed in downstream pulping
   - Twin benefit: Recovered water can be cascaded to earlier wash stages
   - Expected water recovery: **15,000-20,000 m³/month**

5. **Brownstock Washing Optimization**
   - Multi-stage washing with counter-current configuration
   - Apply only necessary wash water per stage based on dissolved carbohydrate concentration
   - Expected savings: **10,000-15,000 m³/month**

**Long-term (Month 9-12):**
6. **Closed-Loop Depithing Process**
   - Capture depither suspension discharge → Centrifugal separation
   - Solids → Pith disposal / Biomass energy
   - Liquor → Recycled as depither makeup water (50-60% displacement of fresh water)
   - Expected displacement: **30,000-35,000 m³/month** (from 91,000 current usage)

### Total Bagasse Pulping Water Reduction: **60,000-80,000 m³/month** (6-8.8% of Bagasse total, ~2.4-3.2% overall)
**Specific Water Consumption Reduction: 0.9-1.2 m³/tonne**

## 5. COOLING WATER SYSTEMS - MAKE-UP WATER REDUCTION

### Current Status:
- **Current Cooling Tower Makeup Water:** 65,000 m³/month (coded as "Others-Cooling water etc.")
- **Cooling Tower Blow-down:** Estimated at 20-30% of recirculation rate
- **Operating Cycles of Concentration:** 2.0-2.5 (industry standard ~3.0-3.5 is optimal)

### Issues Identified:
1. **Low CoC (Cycles of Concentration):** Excessive blow-down required → Higher makeup water
2. **Uncontrolled blow-down:** No automatic conductivity control
3. **Inadequate water conditioning:** Scale formation → Reduced efficiency → Increased makeup
4. **No makeup water alternative sources:** 100% fresh river water used

### Action Items:

**Immediate (Month 1-2):**
1. **Cooling Tower Performance Audit**
   - Measure cooling tower inlet/outlet temperatures, flow rate, makeup water rate
   - Establish baseline: Current makeup rate estimated as **65,000 m³/month**
   - Industry benchmark for similar mills: **40-50,000 m³/month**
   - **Efficiency gap: 15,000-25,000 m³/month (23-38% excess)**

2. **Automatic Conductivity-Based Blow-down Control**
   - Install online conductivity sensor with proportional blow-down valve
   - Target Conductivity: Increase from current state to 2,500-3,000 ppm (vs. typical 1,500-2,000 ppm)
   - Raise CoC from 2.0-2.5 → 3.0-3.5
   - Expected blow-down reduction: **30-40% decrease** in makeup water
   - **Savings: 20,000-26,000 m³/month**
   - **Reduction in m³/tonne: 0.3-0.39 m³/tonne**

3. **Water Conditioning Program**
   - Implement advanced polyamine + polymer dispersant treatment (as already practiced for boiler, extend to cooling tower)
   - Target: Eliminate scale formation, reduce cleaning cycles from 2 years to 3-4 years
   - Expected fouling factor improvement: **10-15%** heat transfer efficiency gain
   - Effect: Operate at higher CoC safely → Further blow-down reduction of **8-12%**

**Short-term (Month 3-9):**
4. **Alternative Makeup Water Sources**
   - Route RO reject water from DM plant (40-50,000 m³/month) → Cooling tower makeup
   - Route degraded reuse water from drainage recovery (100,000+ m³/month available) → Cooling tower
   - Expected displacement of fresh river water: **35,000-45,000 m³/month**
   - **Reduction in m³/tonne: 0.52-0.67 m³/tonne**

5. **Air-Cooled Heat Exchanger Retrofit (Selective)**
   - For low-demand cooling circuits (e.g., DM plant cooling, not main process cooling)
   - Reduces cooling tower system load by 10-15% → Proportional reduction in makeup
   - Expected makeup water reduction: **8,000-10,000 m³/month**

6. **Evaporative Cooler in Series (Summer Strategy)**
   - Deploy wet-bulb evaporative coolers pre-cooling for main cooling tower inlet
   - Reduces inlet temperature → Lower outlet temperature needed → Extended cooling tower cycles
   - Seasonal application (Apr-Oct, 7 months) expected savings: **12,000-15,000 m³/month**

**Long-term (Month 10-12):**
7. **Closed-Loop Cooling for Non-Critical Loads**
   - Install isolated plate heat exchanger loops for:
     - Compressor cooling
     - Control panel cooling
     - DM plant air cooling
   - Reduces cooling tower load by 8-10% → Makeup reduction of **5,000-7,000 m³/month**

### Total Cooling Water Makeup Reduction: **70,000-95,000 m³/month** reduction potential (estimated from 65,000 current → 0-30,000 target)
**Even conservative scenario:** Reduce from 65,000 → 45,000 m³/month = **20,000 m³/month saved (30.8% reduction)**
**Reduction in m³/tonne: 0.3-1.4 m³/tonne**

## 9. CIRCULAR ECONOMY - REUSE & RECYCLING STRATEGIES

### Strategic Framework:

The following cascading water recycling model maximizes water reuse through 3-4 stages of quality degradation:

```
STAGE 1: Highest Quality (Direct Reuse)
├─ DM Water → Boiler feedwater (after single cycle) → Route to cooling tower / low-grade wash
├─ RO Permeate → High-quality industrial use
└─ Hot condensate → Heat extraction → Reuse as makeup water

STAGE 2: Medium Quality (Treatment + Reuse)
├─ Collected Drainage from Pulping → MBR treatment → Paper machine, cooling tower
├─ Paper Machine Condensate → Cooling + Polishing → DM plant feed or dust suppression
└─ Greywater from Domestic → Basic filtration → Toilet flushing, urinal, landscape

STAGE 3: Low Quality (Pre-treatment Required)
├─ Bagasse Pulping Effluent → Settling + MBR → Irrigation / recharge ponds
├─ Chemical Recovery Discharge → Temperature reduction + MBR → Bagasse process makeup
└─ Cooling Tower Blowdown → Holding pond → Evaporation / soil irrigation

STAGE 4: Disposal/Regeneration (Energy Recovery)
└─ Final Brine & Irretrievable Waste → Evaporation ponds / controlled discharge
```
